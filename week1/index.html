Pike Street's Rouladen

Prep Time: 25 min Cook Time: 1 hr 40 min

Servings: 4

Source: Razzledazzlerecipes.com

Ingredients:

  • 4 pieces top serloin of beef, thinly sliced about 1/8 inch thick
  • 4 pieces of bacon, thin cut
  • 4 thin slices of prosciutto
  • 4 thin dill pickle spears
  • 1 white onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup good mustard
  • 2-3 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 sprig of fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1/2 t. paprika
  • 1/2 lb. sliced mushrooms
  • 1 T. porcini powder
  • 1/2 C. dry red wine
  • 1 C. water or beef stock
  • salt and pepper
  • cornstarch
  • sour cream for serving

Directions

Prep for assembly

  1. Put salt, pepper, chopped onion, and mustard in separate bowls.
  2. Count out two toothpicks and one pickle spear for each roll you are making.

This will help avoid cross contamination as you assemble the rolls.

Assemble rolls

Working individually:

  1. Lay meat slices out flat. You might need to pound out thicker slices.
  2. Sprinkle with salt and pepper. Use salt sparingly as you can adjust the gravy before serving.
  3. With a silicone pastry brush, spread a thin coat of mustard on top.
  4. Sprinkle with chopped onion. You can use as much or as little as you want. I use about 1 T. per slice.
  5. Add a slice of prosciutto.
  6. Place a pickle spear on one end of the meat and roll it up.
  7. Wrap roll with a slice of bacon.
  8. Secure with two toothpicks: one on each end of the bacon, pushing the toothpick all the way through. This keeps the rolls together and keeps the bacon on.

Cook

  1. In a skillet, heat about 2 T. of oil and brown the rolls on all sides. Remove to a plate.
  2. Add bay leaves, rosemary, paprika, red wine, and water/beef stock to the skillet.
  3. Return the rolls and any juices from the plate. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer for 90 minutes.
  4. Thicken the gravy with cornstarch slurry.
  5. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Finish with a teaspoon of sour cream on top.
